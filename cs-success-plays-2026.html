CS Success Plays

Trigger-based playbook for growth, retention, and lifecycle management

Updated: March 2026

How This Works

Each play is built around a trigger -- a signal that something is changing in a customer's business, team, or strategy. When you spot a trigger, run the play.

ElementWhat It Tells You
TriggerThe signal that activates the play. What you see in HubSpot, LinkedIn, tickets, or conversation.
OpportunityWhat you can gain by acting: expansion, retention, advocacy, deeper relationship.
RiskWhat you lose by not acting: churn, revenue loss, competitor foothold.
ActionsStep-by-step. What to do, in what order, and who to involve.
MessagingCopy-paste templates you can personalize. Tested language that works.
ChannelWhere to reach out: email, call, LinkedIn, QBR, or a combination.

G1

Customer Health = Green

Growth & Advocacy

Trigger
Customer health score is Green. Account is stable, engaged, and satisfied.

Opportunity

  • Reinforce value and satisfaction before complacency sets in
  • Explore upsell or expansion opportunities (new countries, products)
  • Capture advocacy: testimonials, case studies, referrals

Risk

  • Green does not mean "forever" -- value may be fragile if not actively reinforced
  • Quiet champions may change roles without notice (see POC Change play)
  • Competitor outreach targets satisfied accounts who are not being proactively engaged

Actions

  1. Celebrate recent wins -- acknowledge milestones, headcount growth, or successful onboardings
  2. Explore what's next -- new markets, new features, deeper adoption. Ask about their roadmap.
  3. Ask for advocacy -- testimonials, case studies, or referrals if timing is right
  4. Keep them informed -- share relevant country/market insights, new product updates
  5. Schedule QBR or monthly check-in to maintain cadence

Messaging Template

Hi [Customer Name],

Loving the momentum your team has right now -- thanks for being such a strong partner.

Even though things are going well, I'd love the chance to connect and discuss some untapped potential around savings, growth, and possible new markets. I also want to realign on goals. Promise it will be worth your time.

When's your next available 30 mins?

Alternatively, my calendar [HERE] is updated -- feel free to book a time that works best.
Channel: Email or Video Call
G2

Expansion Signals

EEs with other vendors / consolidation opportunity

Trigger
Customer has EEs managed by another vendor, or you learn they're using multiple EOR providers across regions.

Opportunity

  • Consolidate EEs under one vendor for simplification of global workforce management
  • Reduce client admin overhead and improve consistency
  • Increase revenue per account

Risk

  • Lose EEs to competitor if they consolidate away from us
  • Client may not see the value of switching providers for existing EEs

Actions

  1. Inquire about their full global hiring setup -- don't assume you know all vendors involved
  2. Ask about contractors -- are they part of the mix? Misclassification risk is a lever
  3. Position consolidation as simplification -- one partner, one invoice, one compliance layer

Messaging Templates

I'd love to get a better sense of your global hiring setup. Are you currently working with multiple vendors or handling things region by region?

Are contractors part of your mix, or are you mostly focused on full-time employees?

It could be worth exploring whether there's an opportunity to simplify things under one partner to reduce admin and improve consistency. Would you be open to that?
Channel: Check-in / Monthly / QBR Call
G3

Open Roles Detected

LinkedIn job postings open 15+ days

Trigger
Customer has open roles on LinkedIn for 15+ days -- either US-based or global.

Scenario A: US-Based Hiring

Opportunity
Start a conversation about global expansion. Position Remote People as a partner to scale hiring efforts globally and optimize costs and compliance.
Risk
Customer may grow solely in the US with a local entity, missing global talent pools and cost savings.

Action: Reach out to explore whether the customer is open to accessing global talent to reduce hiring costs and time-to-hire.

Hi [Customer Name],

I wanted to reach out and see how your hiring efforts are progressing.

I noticed you announced [Job opening in the US] a little over 2 weeks ago and wanted to offer support -- whether with candidate sourcing or, if you're considering expanding your search globally, I can recommend a few countries that could fill the requirement.
Channel: Email or LinkedIn Message, then follow-up call

Scenario B: Global Hiring

Opportunity
Engage immediately to support compliance, speed, and cost-effectiveness in international hiring.
Risk
Customer may be using another EOR or engaging talent in non-compliant ways (e.g., contractors where employment is more appropriate).

Action: Watch for international job postings. Research internally to confirm if Remote People is involved. If not, explore why and how you can help.

Hi [Customer Name],

I wanted to reach out and see how your global hiring efforts are progressing.

I noticed you recently announced [Job opening in country/countries] about 2 weeks ago and wanted to offer my support.

We'd be happy to provide a shortlist of top [role] candidates in [countries] if it helps.
Channel: Email or LinkedIn Message, then follow-up call

G5

Multi-Country Growth

Active EEs in multiple countries

Trigger
Customer has active EEs in multiple countries. They're already global -- now it's about expanding the footprint.

Opportunity

  • Expand footprint into countries they're active in but not partnering with us
  • Simplify management -- EOR is clearly a key piece of their business strategy
  • Use success in one country to suggest "copy-paste" strategy in similar markets

Risk

  • Increased EE density in one country could signal future entity setup (Rule of 5)
  • They may be working with competitors in countries we don't know about

Actions

  1. Ask about countries not currently managed by Remote People
  2. Explore countries they'd like to break into
  3. Share insights on similar hiring markets (e.g., "Customers hiring in Mexico often also succeed in Colombia or Argentina")

Messaging Templates

I noticed we're supporting your team in [X, Y, Z countries]. Curious -- are there other markets where you're active but not currently partnering with us?
Your team's growth in [Country] has been great to watch -- it's a clear signal you're doing something right!

Given your current growth, have you considered replicating the approach in other regions? There are a few countries where we can help you access similar talent profiles with lower cost and reduced employer burden. Happy to share some recommendations.

Would you be open to exploring what that might look like?
Channel: Check-in / QBR call or Email
G6

Referral Ask

Leverage strong relationships for warm introductions

Trigger
Customer is referral-ready: Green health, recent success, NPS promoter, post-QBR momentum, or recent expansion.

Opportunity

  • Expand pipeline through warm introductions
  • Build customer advocacy and long-term loyalty
  • Higher quality prospects who already trust the referrer

Risk

  • Waiting too long and missing the momentum of customer satisfaction
  • Asking too early (before value is felt) weakens the relationship
  • Referrals go to competitors if you don't ask

Actions

  1. Identify referral-ready customers (Green health, recent success, NPS promoters, post-QBR moments, expansions)
  2. Ask during moments of high value or gratitude
  3. Make the ask specific, low-effort, and personalized
  4. Follow up with gratitude, status updates, and appreciation

Messaging Template

Hi [Customer Name],

I've really enjoyed seeing how your team has grown in [country/countries]. Your journey is a great example of how global hiring can work when done right.

We've been growing our team to enable a wider range of global services, and I'm starting to speak with others just beginning to explore international hiring.

If there's anyone in your network who's curious about how to scale globally, your endorsement would carry significant weight, and I would be grateful for any referrals you could provide.

As a thank you, we'd be happy to offer you 10% off one month of service fees (max $1,500) once your referral makes their first hire. But more than anything, we just appreciate your support.

Thanks for being such a thoughtful partner.
Channel: Email or Call

R1

Customer Health = Yellow

Risk & Retention

Trigger
Customer health score is Yellow. Momentum is slowing, engagement may be dipping, or unresolved friction is building.

Opportunity

  • Prevent risk from becoming critical
  • Deepen the relationship with proactive support
  • Uncover blockers early and demonstrate value

Risk

  • Customer is unsure of long-term fit
  • Momentum is slowing, leading to possible disengagement
  • One bad experience could tip the balance toward churn

Actions

  1. Schedule a check-in to explore friction points
  2. Reiterate goals and share a tailored optimization plan
  3. Highlight quick wins and recent value delivered
  4. Ask for feedback to address hesitations early

Would love to connect and talk through how things are going and how we can best support you in the weeks ahead.

When's your next available 30 mins?

Alternatively, my calendar [HERE] is updated -- feel free to book a time that works best.
Channel: Email, then Video Call
R2

Customer Health = Red

Critical risk -- act now

Trigger
Customer health score is Red. Churn is imminent if action isn't taken immediately.

Opportunity

  • Rebuild trust and realign quickly before churn
  • Identify root causes and implement a turnaround plan
  • Demonstrate accountability and high-touch support

Risk

  • Churn is imminent if action isn't taken
  • Customer may be evaluating competitors or already transitioning away
  • Reputation risk if poor experience is shared internally or externally

Actions

  1. Escalate internally for exec visibility and support
  2. Create a recovery plan with clear owners and timelines
  3. Schedule 1:1 call with customer to review the plan
  4. Increase touchpoint frequency temporarily until stabilized

Messaging Template

Hi [Customer Name],

I've seen a few signals that things might not be going as smoothly as we'd like.

I'd love to connect and propose a recovery plan we can collaborate on.

When's your next available 30 mins?

Alternatively, my calendar [HERE] is updated -- feel free to book a time that works best.
Channel: Video Call + follow-up email summary
Escalation Required
Red health accounts with renewal within 90 days must be escalated to Head of CS with a risk brief: ARR at risk, root cause, recovery actions taken, and recommended exec intervention.
R3

POC Change

New decision-maker or contact change

Trigger
Primary point of contact leaves, changes roles, or a new decision-maker is introduced.

Opportunity

Rebuild trust and establish yourself as a strategic partner to the new contact.

Risk

Lose influence, or new POC prefers other vendors. 30 to 40% of churned accounts had a POC change in the prior 6 months.

Actions

  1. Respond within 24 hours of the introduction
  2. Research the new POC on LinkedIn (background, communication style signals)
  3. Prepare a one-page account summary (EEs, countries, products, key dates, open items, recent wins)
  4. Schedule an onboarding call within 5 business days
  5. Listen first (60%), share account summary (20%), establish communication preferences (20%)

Messaging Template

Welcome aboard! I'd love to hear more about your goals and how we can support your success in this new role.

Happy to set up some time to chat so I can walk you through everything we have in place and hear about your priorities.
Channel: Email, then intro call
R4

Support Ticket Trends

Rising volume or recurring issues

Trigger
Ticket volume rising, recurring issue categories, or client sentiment flagged as negative.

Opportunity

Proactively address root causes, improve CSAT and retention. Show the client you're monitoring their experience.

Risk

Persistent issues cause churn or loss of trust. Unaddressed tickets erode the relationship silently.

Actions

  1. Analyze tickets -- pull volume, categories, resolution times, repeat issues
  2. Reach out proactively before the client escalates
  3. Propose a joint action plan with root cause fixes and timelines
  4. Coordinate internally with the Support team lead

Messaging Template

I noticed a few recent tickets related to [issue]. Would it help if we jumped on a call to work through the root cause and find a long-term fix?
Channel: Email, then Video Call
R5

Entity Setup Risk

Customer setting up their own entity / high EE concentration

Trigger
Customer confirms or signals entity setup in a country, OR has high EE density in a single country (Rule of 5+).

Opportunity

  • Proactively offer support with global payroll and value-added services (office setup, IT provisioning) to retain revenue
  • Position Remote People as a partner beyond EOR -- entity setup fast track, ongoing payroll, compliance support
  • Remain embedded as a partner even as the relationship evolves

Risk

Customer transitions away completely after setting up their own entity, leading to loss of headcount and revenue.

Actions

  1. Reach out when entity setup is confirmed or suspected
  2. Offer continued support -- global payroll, office setup, IT provisioning
  3. Walk them through the entity setup fast track program
  4. For multi-country accounts, reinforce value of EOR in countries where entity doesn't make sense

Messaging Template

Congrats on the new entity! That's a huge milestone, [Customer Name].

We understand this is a natural transition and wanted to let you know that we're set up to support you beyond EOR and into your own entity.

As you make this transition, global payroll, office setup, and IT provisioning can be a challenge. We can help keep things simple and continue to assist you in [Country].

Happy to walk you through our entity setup fast track program.
Channel: Email or Call

L3

Schedule QBR

Quarterly or semi-annual business review

Trigger
Quarterly cadence for Tier 1, semi-annual for Tier 2. Also triggered for at-risk accounts or accounts with low recent engagement.

Opportunity

  • Strengthen strategic alignment and customer trust
  • Showcase value delivered to date
  • Uncover growth, retention, or upsell opportunities
  • Reignite engagement with less active accounts
  • Gather feedback that shapes product/service improvements

Risk

  • Lack of visibility into sentiment may lead to silent churn
  • Stakeholders may not see the value of the meeting if poorly prepared
  • Missed opportunity to correct issues or prevent quiet disengagement

Actions

  1. Schedule proactively (quarterly or semi-annually by tier)
  2. Use a structured, value-driven agenda
  3. Prepare personalized insights and outcomes ahead of time
  4. Realign on goals, capture feedback, define next steps

Messaging Template

As part of our ongoing commitment to your success, let's schedule your next Quarterly Business Review.

This session is a chance for us to reflect on how things have gone so far, hear what's happening in your world, share what's new on our side, and plan for what's next. Promise it will be worth your time.

Could you let me know your availability in the next 2 weeks? I'm happy to accommodate what works best. If you prefer, you can schedule directly [here].
Channel: Email or Call
